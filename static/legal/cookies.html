Cookie Notice

This Cookie Notice explains how Atlas IP Holdings LLC (“Atlas”, “we”, “us”, “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies on the BRidge platform, websites, and web applications (collectively, “BRidge”). It forms part of the Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

Summary: We use cookies to make BRidge work securely, remember preferences, measure usage, and improve performance. You can manage non-essential cookies via our banner or your browser settings. We honor applicable opt-out signals (e.g., GPC).

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help operate websites and collect information about your activity. Similar technologies include local storage, pixels, and beacons.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential (Required): enable login, session security, CSRF protection, and load balancing. These are necessary for core functionality and cannot be disabled.
  • Functional: remember preferences (e.g., language, time zone, display options) to personalize your experience.
  • Analytics & Performance: collect aggregated usage and performance metrics (e.g., request volume, latency, error rates). We use privacy-respecting analytics and do not track you across third-party sites.
  • Marketing (Optional): used sparingly for Atlas announcements or opt-in communications, if enabled.

2.1 First- vs Third-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by Atlas/BRidge. In limited cases, we may allow third-party cookies for strictly necessary functions (e.g., transactional email pixeling or performance analytics) and they are subject to those providers’ policies.

3. Managing Your Preferences

  • Use our on-site banner or preferences page to accept/reject non-essential cookies. Manage choices at /privacy/choices (where available).
  • You can delete or block cookies via your browser settings. Essential cookies are required for basic platform functionality and cannot be turned off.
  • We honor legally recognized opt-out preference signals (e.g., Global Privacy Control) in supported jurisdictions.
